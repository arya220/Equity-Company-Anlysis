## 📋 KEY OUTPUT SECTIONS

### 1. Executive Summary

Cyient is a Hyderabad-based engineering and technology solutions company with ₹7,328Cr TTM revenue across DET (digital/engineering), DLM (manufacturing, separately listed), and a nascent semiconductor business. At CMP ₹870, Cyient trades at P/E 17.8x — the cheapest in the entire ER&D peer group — reflecting severe margin compression (18%→15%→12–14% OPM), stalled revenue growth (TTM 2%), massive FII exodus (37%→18%), and a loss-making semiconductor venture. The company scores 76/135 on fundamentals (AVOID) driven by weak trigger identification (no formal guidance, "recovery" rather than "growth" thesis), declining margins, and mixed management delivery. The technical override (3/15) pushes this to STRONG AVOID. **While the valuation is genuinely cheap (P/B 1.7x, dividend yield 2.8%), the fundamental quality metrics — ROCE 17% and declining, ROE 13%, CFO/EBITDA 55–69%, margin compression — do not justify bottom-fishing when both LTTS and even Axiscades offer better fundamental profiles.** Cyient is a recovery bet, not a quality compounder.

### 2. Key Strengths

1. **Cheapest in peer group:** P/E 17.8x, EV/EBITDA ~10x, P/B 1.7x — all at deep discounts to own history and peers. Significant margin of safety IF recovery materializes.
2. **Clean balance sheet:** Near debt-free (D/E ~2%). Deleveraging from ₹1,218Cr (FY23) to ₹436Cr (Sep 2025).
3. **Dividend discipline:** 50.5% average payout, 2.8% yield at CMP. Rare in mid-cap IT.
4. **DII accumulation:** DIIs have increased from 21% to 38% in 2 years — domestic institutions are building positions at these levels.
5. **DET order intake:** $836M in FY25 across 24 large deals. Key accounts grew 15.5% YoY in Q3 FY26.

### 3. Key Risks

1. **FII capitulation:** FII holding collapsed from 36.57% (Jun 2023) to 17.56% (Sep 2025). This is not noise — it signals deep institutional concern about growth trajectory.
2. **Margin compression:** OPM declined from 18% (FY24) to 15% (FY25) to 12–14% (recent quarters). DET EBIT at 12.4% in Q3 FY26. Semiconductor segment loss-making.
3. **Stalled growth:** TTM revenue growth only 2%. Q3 FY26 revenue -4% YoY. DET CC revenue -1.9% YoY.
4. **Low promoter holding:** 23.28% — among the lowest in the IT sector. No M&A defense. Vulnerable to activist pressure.
5. **Semiconductor venture uncertainty:** Kinetic Technologies acquisition ($93M) adds capabilities but margin-dilutive. EBIT neutral target by FY27 is aspirational.

### 4. Thesis Breakers

1. FY26 full-year OPM falls below 13% (would signal structural margin destruction)
2. CFO/EBITDA drops below 50% for FY26
3. Semiconductor segment losses exceed ₹100Cr annually
4. Promoter holding declines below 22%
5. DET order intake declines >15% YoY

### 5. Conviction Builders (what would trigger upgrade to MONITOR/BUY)

1. DET EBIT margin recovery to >14% for 2 consecutive quarters
2. FY27 revenue growth guidance >10% (if provided)
3. Semiconductor segment reaching EBIT neutral
4. FII buying resumption (any quarter showing net FII increase)
5. Stage 1 base formation with 30W MA flattening + price holding ₹850+ for 8–10 weeks
