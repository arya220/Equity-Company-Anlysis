## WHY GUJARAT GAS IS LOWER PRIORITY THAN MGL

1. **49% industrial exposure** vs MGL's 16% — Gujarat Gas is far more cyclical and exposed to the Gas Supply Regulation Order 2026 (10% volume impact per Nomura).
2. **P/E 25x** vs MGL's 8.6x — Gujarat Gas is 3x more expensive.
3. **OPM 16%** vs MGL's 22% — lower margins despite higher industrial mix.
4. Post-merger complexity (GSPC + GSPL integration) adds execution risk.
5. The competitive advantage (Gujarat industrial demand) is the same feature that makes it vulnerable in supply disruption scenarios.

**Assessment:** Gujarat Gas would likely score in the 80-88/135 range (MONITOR). But at P/E 25x, the risk/reward is materially worse than MGL at 8.6x. Between the two Gujarat players (Gujarat Gas as CGD + GSPL as pipeline), GSPL might be more interesting as a pure pipeline infrastructure play — but that's a separate analysis.

**Verdict: DEPRIORITIZED** — MGL is the superior CGD play. Gujarat Gas at P/E 25x doesn't justify deep-dive analysis time when MGL is available at 8.6x.

**Re-check: May 2026** — only if P/E compresses to <18x or if industrial volume recovery is visible post-Hormuz.
