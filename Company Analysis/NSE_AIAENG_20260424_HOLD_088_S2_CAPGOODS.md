## 🌐 PHASE 0.5: THEMATIC ASSESSMENT

### 0.5.1 Theme Identification — 2 pts

**Theme:** Global conversion of mining grinding media from forged steel balls to high-chrome grinding media — a structural efficiency/TCO upgrade, not cyclical. Cement grinding internals is a mature adjacency; mining is the growth theme.

**Structural driver:** High-chrome media offers 2.5–3x lifespan vs forged steel + lower specific energy consumption + better downstream recovery rates. As ore grades decline globally (especially copper, gold), miners are forced toward efficiency gains. The penetration opportunity is real but the conversion timeline is owned by mine operators, not AIA.

Score: **2/2** — structurally defined, not cyclical.

🔒 **LOCK Score: 2/2 | Source: CRISIL rating rationale Jul 2025, AR FY24**

---

### 0.5.2 Theme Maturity & Penetration — 3 pts

Per CRISIL: High-chrome grinding media accounts for **~20% of the ~3 million tonne global grinding media market**. AIA has a ~30% share of the high-chrome segment but the addressable conversion runway (80% still on forged steel) is large in theory. However, the conversion has been in progress for 15+ years and adoption pace is slow — miners resist switching costs. This places the theme in early-mid innings by global penetration but late in terms of AIA's own historical growth delivery (5Y sales CAGR only 7.6%).

Score: **2/3** — Mid innings by penetration; slow adoption despite multi-year runway; tempered by AIA's own decelerating growth.

🔒 **LOCK Score: 2/3 | Source: CRISIL rating rationale Jul 2025**

---

### 0.5.3 Global Precedent Check — 2 pts

**Global precedent:** Magotteaux (Belgium, now part of Sigdo Koppers, Chile) — the global #1 in high-chrome grinding media. It scaled the same thesis in Latin America and parts of Europe over 20+ years. Enabling conditions: low ore grades, large mining customers, long-term contracts.

**KEY DIFFERENCES:**
1. **AIA is export-dependent (~65% exports)** — subject to freight volatility, FX risk, and trade actions (US anti-dumping 9.16%, Brazil CVD determination). Magotteaux benefits from local manufacturing near mines.
2. **AIA's conversion cycle is customer-owned and long.** Mine operators conduct trials that span 12–24 months, often with no guaranteed conversion.
3. **Competitor Magotteaux is also AIA's customer in select markets** — this is a duopoly, not a monopoly; pricing pressure exists.
4. Ghana/China local manufacturing is AIA's response to this gap but plants are 18–24 months from commissioning.

Score: **1/2** — Precedent exists and is being actively pursued, but structural differences (logistics, cycle length, duopoly pricing) are material.

🔒 **LOCK Score: 1/2 | Source: CRISIL rating rationale, Q2/Q3FY26 concall summaries**

### 0.5.5 Competitive Landscape Evolution — 1 pt

The high-chrome grinding media market is a global **duopoly** between AIA (India) and Magotteaux (now Sigdo Koppers). This is structurally consolidated. AIA is among the top 2 globally — category leader position is intact. However, in FY25 AIA "lost some volume to competitors including a significant customer partly due to duty structure uncertainties" per Q4FY25 concall summary — meaning share loss is occurring.

Score: **1/1** — Consolidated duopoly, but AIA has given back some share recently; net position still top-2.

### 0.5.6 Theme Risk Assessment — 2 pts

**What kills/stalls the theme:**
1. **Ore grade stability** — if miners find alternative efficiency paths (HPGR tech, bio-leaching), high-chrome media demand plateaus
2. **Freight cost structural inflation** — AIA's export model becomes increasingly less competitive vs local producers in destination markets
3. **Trade barriers** — rising protectionism (US, Brazil already active; EU a risk) accelerates the shift to local production, hurting AIA's India-centric model

**Early warning signals:** Volume growth below 5% for 4+ consecutive quarters (already present in FY25); rising duty filings across geographies (happening).

Score: **1/2** — Theme is durable but has two identifiable risks (trade barriers, freight) actively materializing within the 3–5Y horizon.
