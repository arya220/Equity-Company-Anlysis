## PHASE 1: BUSINESS ORIENTATION — 18/20

### 1.1 Business Model Clarity — 5/5
MGL distributes CNG (71% of volumes) and PNG (29% — domestic + industrial/commercial) in Mumbai, adjoining areas, Raigad, and 6 geographical areas post-UEPL acquisition. Revenue model: gas procurement cost + regulated margin (₹9-10.50/scm EBITDA guidance). 491 CNG stations, 8,182 km pipeline, 3.07M PNG household connections, 1.25M+ CNG vehicles registered. Natural monopoly — sole authorized distributor in its GAs.

Mumbai is the most lucrative CGD geography in India — highest population density, highest CNG vehicle concentration, and highest willingness to pay. Pricing power demonstrated through CNG price hikes passed on to consumers.
🔒 LOCK: **5/5** | Source: ICRA Report Nov 2025

## 🔴 RED TEAM CHECK

**Step 1 — Bear Case:**
MGL's margins have contracted from 30% to 17% in just 6 quarters as LNG procurement costs surged, and the Hormuz crisis threatens to make this structural rather than cyclical. The CNG volume growth thesis faces a medium-term existential risk from EV adoption — Mumbai is India's most EV-ready city with the densest charging infra, and the government could pivot subsidies from CNG to EVs at any point. At ₹939 the stock is -41% from its peak, and the 52W low of ₹900 has not been conclusively defended — a break below could trigger another 15-20% downside to ₹750-780.

**Step 2 — Biggest Assumption:**
"The bull case assumes that: **LNG procurement costs normalize within 2-3 quarters (Hormuz resolution or alternative sourcing) and operating margins recover to the 22-25% range.**"

**Step 3 — Stress Test:**
- Probability of failure: 30%
- Implied price if margins stay at 17%: ₹720 (8x compressed EPS of ₹90)
