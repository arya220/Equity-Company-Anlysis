## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Anant Raj is a 50-year-old NCR-focused real estate developer pivoting aggressively into data centers via wholly-owned subsidiary Anant Raj Cloud Private Limited. The data center theme is Tier 1 (India DC penetration <20%, $70–80B investment opportunity by 2030), and the company has a genuine first-mover cost advantage with 28 MW operational capacity, a 63 MW Dec-2026 target, and 117 MW by FY28 — funded entirely from residential cash flows and a ₹1,100 Cr QIP.

**However, the investment case fails the Hard Disqualification Layer.** Consolidated CFO/EBITDA has been below 30% for 3 consecutive years (FY23: 17%, FY24: −8%, FY25: 20%). While the real estate sector structurally produces lumpy CFO, and the company has been reinvesting residential cash flows into data center infrastructure while simultaneously deleveraging (debt reduced from ₹1,400 Cr to ₹426 Cr over 4 years), the framework rule is explicit and non-negotiable: **CFO/EBITDA < 30% for 3+ consecutive years → AUTOMATIC STRONG AVOID.**

**Verdict: STRONG AVOID (Hard DQ)** | Score: Not scored (Hard DQ override) | Theme: 10/12 (Tier 1)

The data center re-rating narrative is compelling — but the CFO/EBITDA gate exists precisely to catch situations where narrative overwhelms cash generation arithmetic. This stock warrants priority re-check given the Tier 1 theme designation.

### 0.5.1 Theme Identification & Definition — 2/2

**Theme:** India's data center infrastructure build-out, driven by cloud migration, AI compute demand, data localization regulations (DPDP Act 2023), and sovereign cloud mandates.

**Structural driver:** Enterprise/government cloud adoption at <20% penetration; data sovereignty regulations requiring in-country hosting; AI workload explosion requiring GPU/HPC infrastructure.

### 0.5.2 Theme Maturity & Penetration — 3/3

- India DC capacity: ~1.5 GW currently vs estimated 3 GW needed by 2030
- Organized colocation penetration: <15% of enterprise IT workloads
- India is 10–15 years behind the US/China in DC capacity per capita
- Industry expected to grow at 20% CAGR to 2030; $70–80 billion investment needed
- Runway: 8–10 years minimum of high growth before maturity

🔒 Score: **3/3** | Source: Industry estimates (Yotta, JLL India DC Market Report), CRISIL

### 0.5.3 Global Precedent Check — 1/2

**Global precedent:** US data center market where Equinix, Digital Realty scaled from sub-GW to multi-GW capacity over 15+ years. Equinix trades at 25–30x EV/EBITDA. China hyperscale DC build-out similarly scaled over 10 years.

**Enabling conditions:** Reliable power supply, fiber backbone, regulatory clarity, enterprise adoption. India has these partially — power availability is a constraint in many locations; Anant Raj's NCR locations have grid access.

**KEY DIFFERENCES:**
- US DCs scaled on the back of hyperscaler demand (AWS/Azure/GCP) which is only nascent in India
- India's power cost and reliability differ materially from US; Anant Raj relies on grid + backup DG
- Land cost dynamics differ — Anant Raj's pre-existing land bank is a unique India advantage but not replicable
- US DC operators are pure-play with 80%+ recurring revenue; Anant Raj is hybrid real estate + DC, creating conglomerate discount risk

🔒 Score: **1/2** | Source: Equinix financials, industry reports

### 0.5.4 Policy & Regulatory Tailwind — 2/2

- **MeitY empanelment** for sovereign cloud services — expected/received, enabling government contracts
- **DPDP Act 2023** mandates data localization for certain categories
- **National Data Centre Policy** provides incentive framework
- **IT/ITeS SEZ benefits** applicable to Anant Raj's tech parks
- Risk of policy reversal: Low. Bipartisan support for digital infrastructure

🔒 Score: **2/2** | Source: MeitY policy documents, BSE filings

### 0.5.5 Competitive Landscape Evolution — 1/1

- Top players: NTT, STT GDC, Adani, Yotta, CtrlS, Nxtra (Bharti), Anant Raj
- Market consolidating around 5–7 organized players
- High barriers: land + power + capital + compliance
- Anant Raj has unique cost advantage — pre-existing buildings in NCR locations reduce capex by 30–40% vs greenfield competitors

🔒 Score: **1/1** | Source: Industry reports, Q2 FY26 concall

### 0.5.6 Theme Risk Assessment — 1/2

**What kills/stalls the theme:**
- Global AI spending slowdown reducing hyperscaler demand (probability: 15–20% in 3Y horizon)
- Power availability constraints in NCR limiting DC expansion (probability: 20–25%)
- Technology shift (edge computing reducing centralized DC demand) — long-term risk, not near-term
- Oversupply in NCR from multiple players building simultaneously

**Early warning:** Occupancy rates falling below 70% across industry, hyperscaler capex cuts

Theme is durable but has 1–2 identifiable risks (power constraints + potential oversupply).

🔒 Score: **1/2** | Source: Industry analysis
