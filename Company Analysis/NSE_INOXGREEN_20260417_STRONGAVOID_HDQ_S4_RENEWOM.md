## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Inox Green Energy Services (INOXGREEN) is India's only listed pure-play renewable O&M service provider, a subsidiary of Inox Wind Limited (IWL), and part of the INOXGFL group. The company has rapidly scaled its O&M portfolio from ~3.2 GW (Mar 2024) to ~12.5 GW (Sep 2025) through aggressive inorganic expansion, with a further 4.5 GW pending via the Wind World India NCLT acquisition.

**VERDICT: STRONG AVOID — Hard Disqualification Triggered**

The company has diluted equity by ~32.5% over the last 3 years (post-IPO FY23 equity capital ₹292 Cr → current ~₹387 Cr, with additional warrants outstanding taking fully diluted to ₹409 Cr) while consolidated operating EBITDA has DECLINED from ₹57 Cr (FY23) to ₹52 Cr (FY25) and ₹38 Cr TTM. This fires the Hard DQ trigger: *"Equity dilution > 20% in last 3 years without proportionate EBITDA growth."* The analysis halts per framework rules. No score, no matter how compelling the O&M scale-up narrative, overrides this gate.

**Actual FY25 EBITDA:** ₹52 Cr (27% BELOW required level)

**Actual TTM EBITDA:** ₹38 Cr (47% BELOW required level)

**EBITDA has DECLINED** while shares outstanding have increased 25–40%. The Hard DQ fires unambiguously.

### Anti-Narrative Check

The compelling counter-argument is: "The dilution was for acquisitions (I-Fox Windtechnik, Resowi Energy, Wind World O&M) that haven't fully contributed to EBITDA yet — future EBITDA will be much higher."

This is precisely the narrative the Hard DQ is designed to prevent. Per Core Principle #11: *"Themes do not override cash flow."* Per the Analyst Mandate: *"If you find yourself building a compelling narrative that makes you want to override a disqualifier — that impulse IS the signal to stop."*

The framework does not make exceptions for "dilution that will create future EBITDA." The rule is binary: >20% dilution without proportionate EBITDA growth = STRONG AVOID. Period.

### 5.2 Red Flags Beyond Hard DQ

1. **ROCE 2.6%, ROE 1%** — Among the poorest in the sector; capital deployed is not generating adequate returns
2. **Debtor days 279, Working capital days 741** — Extreme and worsening; cash is locked in receivables, not available for operations
3. **Other income ₹144 Cr TTM vs Operating EBITDA ₹38 Cr** — Profits are overwhelmingly from non-core sources; strip out other income and the business is barely profitable operationally
4. **OPM declining sharply** — From 33–48% range (FY22–24) to 10–13% in H1 FY26; management attributes this to "upfront scheduled maintenance expenses" but the magnitude is alarming
5. **6 consecutive years of losses** (FY18–FY23) — Company turned PAT-positive only in FY24 (₹28 Cr) on ₹35 Cr of other income; core operating profitability is weak
6. **P/E 75–113x** — Extreme premium valuation for a company with sub-3% ROCE and declining OPM
7. **Credit rating CRISIL A** — Significantly below AA threshold; not investment-grade for our framework's quality filter
8. **Parent dependency** — Rating explicitly based on parent notch-up framework (support from IWL); standalone credit profile is weaker

### 5.3 Wind World Acquisition Context

The Wind World India acquisition (4.5 GW O&M + 600 MW IPP) via NCLT resolution is the key forward catalyst. If executed well, it could add meaningful EBITDA and justify the dilution retrospectively. However:

- CoC approved Feb 19, 2026 — final NCLT approval still pending

- EBITDA contribution timeline is uncertain
- The framework correctly requires delivered results, not projected outcomes, to clear the dilution Hard DQ
