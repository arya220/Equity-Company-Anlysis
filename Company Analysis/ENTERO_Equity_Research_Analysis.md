## 📋 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Entero Healthcare Solutions is India's third-largest healthcare products distributor by revenue, operating a technology-driven supply chain platform serving 97,600+ retail pharmacies and 3,000+ hospitals across 505 districts through 131 warehouses.** The company has delivered impressive revenue growth (30% 5Y CAGR) and is executing an aggressive inorganic expansion into MedTech distribution.

**However, the company has a catastrophic cash flow problem that makes it uninvestable under this framework.** CFO has been NEGATIVE in every single year of its existence — even as revenue grew nearly 4x from ₹1,350Cr to ₹5,096Cr. The business model requires significant working capital to fund receivables and inventory, and every rupee of growth consumes MORE cash than the EBITDA it generates. This is structurally similar to other distribution businesses that grow revenues without generating cash. The IPO in Feb 2024 essentially funded the working capital gap — and borrowings are already rising again.

**Verdict: STRONG AVOID (Hard DQ + Gate Fail 3/5)**

The stock fails the Pre-Screen Gate (3/5, missing CFO and technicals) AND triggers a Hard DQ (CFO/EBITDA negative for 6 consecutive years). At ₹1,024, the stock trades at ~40x earnings for a business that has never generated positive annual operating cash flow, with FII ownership declining sharply. The Q3 FY26 quarterly OCF of ₹49Cr is encouraging but unproven at annual level — management needs to deliver ₹108Cr+ in a single quarter (Q4 FY26) to meet guidance, which would be unprecedented.

## 📝 ANALYST NOTES

1. **The OCF turnaround story is the key variable.** Q3 FY26 showed ₹49Cr positive OCF — the first meaningfully positive quarter. But 9M FY26 is still -₹8.5Cr, and management needs ₹108Cr+ in Q4 alone to meet guidance. This would be 63% of full-year EBITDA generated in a single quarter's cash flow — an extraordinary claim. If achieved, it would be a genuine inflection point. If missed, it confirms the structural problem.

2. **Distribution businesses in India have a well-documented working capital trap.** Revenue growth in pharma distribution requires funding receivables (pharmacies/hospitals pay in 60 days) and inventory (52 days) while paying suppliers in 31 days. This 80-day cash conversion cycle means the faster you grow, the more cash you burn — until margins are high enough to self-fund the WC cycle. At 4% EBITDA margin, Entero is nowhere near that threshold.

3. **The MedTech pivot is strategically sound but unproven.** MedTech products carry higher margins (devices, IVD, cardiology) which could meaningfully improve the EBITDA margin profile. The ₹1,000Cr MedTech revenue target for FY27 would represent ~15% of total revenue at higher margins. This is the most credible path to cash flow breakeven — but it requires flawless integration of 4+ acquisitions simultaneously.

4. **Valuation is demanding.** At ~40x TTM P/E for a business that has never generated positive annual CFO, the market is pricing in a cash flow turnaround that hasn't happened yet. Even if FY27 delivers ₹150-200Cr OCF (optimistic), the stock is priced for perfection.

5. **Senior management attrition noted.** Sambit Mohanty (President, Institutional Business) resigned effective Mar 31, 2026. One board member (Vipul Desai) also resigned in May 2025. While not unusual for a growing company, these exits warrant monitoring.
