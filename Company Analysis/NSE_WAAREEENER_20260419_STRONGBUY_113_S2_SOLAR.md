## 🌐 PHASE 0.5: THEMATIC ASSESSMENT

### 0.5.1 Theme Identification & Definition — 2/2

**Theme:** India's structural solar manufacturing sovereignty — the build-out of fully integrated domestic solar value chain (polysilicon → ingot → wafer → cell → module → inverter → storage) driven by Atmanirbhar Bharat, climate commitments (500 GW RE by 2030), and China decoupling strategy.

**Structural driver:** Government policy (BCD 40% on modules, 25% on cells since Apr 2022), ALMM-I for modules (mandatory since Apr 2024), ALMM-II for cells (mandatory for utility projects bid after Aug 31, 2025 and open-access post Jun 1, 2026), DCR mandates under PM Surya Ghar and PM-KUSUM, PLI schemes for integrated manufacturing.

🔒 **Score: 2/2** | Source: CARE Ratings Feb 2026, MNRE policy documents

### 0.5.2 Theme Maturity & Penetration — 2/3

India's organized solar manufacturing was 80-85% import-dependent until FY22. Post-ALMM implementation, domestic capacity has scaled from ~4 GW (FY22) to ~74 GW (March 2026 industry-wide). **Industry runs at only 25% capacity utilization** (Premier Energies' CBO statement, Feb 2026) — overcapacity risk is real. However, cell manufacturing (not modules) remains deeply under-penetrated, with ALMM-II mandate just kicking in. Estimated 5-8 years of runway for integrated players like Waaree.

🔒 **Score: 2/3** | Source: Wood Mackenzie forecasts, Premier Energies statements, CARE rationale

### 0.5.3 Global Precedent Check — 1/2

**Precedent:** China's solar industry consolidation (2008-2020) saw Longi, JinkoSolar, Trina emerge as global champions through scale, integration, and policy support. Taiwan's Motech/Neo Solar provide mid-scale precedent for integrated backward integration.

**KEY DIFFERENCES:**
- China had unlimited capital access (state banks); India's solar companies face disciplined balance sheet requirements
- China's demand was dominated by domestic gigascale; India's demand is export-heavy (Waaree's own book is 65% overseas)
- US tariffs specifically target Chinese (not Indian) supply — India is the beneficiary of China+1 positioning, not a fast-follower
- China's consolidation took 12+ years; India's integrated cycle may compress given policy clarity

🔒 **Score: 1/2** | Source: Industry reports, CARE rationale

### 0.5.4 Policy & Regulatory Tailwind — 2/2

Waaree-specific tailwinds quantified and timed:
- **BCD (40% modules, 25% cells)** — active since April 2022
- **ALMM-I for modules** — mandatory since April 1, 2024
- **ALMM-II for cells** — mandatory for utility projects bid after August 31, 2025; open-access post June 1, 2026 (key near-term tailwind)
- **DCR mandate** under PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana & PM-KUSUM Yojana — 40% raw material must be domestic
- **PLI scheme** for backward-integrated plants — Waaree has ₹444 Cr PLI allocated for 1 GW electrolyzer

🔒 **Score: 2/2** | Source: CARE rationale, MNRE schemes

### 0.5.5 Competitive Landscape Evolution — 1/1

Organized private solar manufacturing consolidating around 4-5 major players: **Waaree (22.8 GW module), Premier Energies (11.1 GW module), Vikram Solar (9.5 GW), Adani Solar (~4 GW + expanding), RenewSys**. Waaree is clear #1 with 21% domestic module share and 44% export share (FY24). Entry barriers rising via ALMM certification, policy-compliance complexity, integrated capex (~₹12,000 Cr for Sangam alone).

🔒 **Score: 1/1** | Source: CARE, Nuvama broker note Feb 2026

### 0.5.6 Theme Risk Assessment — 1/2

**What could kill or stall this theme?**

1. **Overcapacity** — India's module capacity doubled to 74 GW in FY25; utilization is 25% industry-wide. Premier Energies' own CBO flagged this in Feb 2026. Could pressure margins if demand doesn't scale in line.
2. **US tariff risk** — Though Waaree has US manufacturing hedge, any reversal of China-specific tariffs or new anti-dumping on Indian modules would impact the 60% international order book.
3. **Policy reversal** — ALMM-II, BCD, or DCR dilution under new government priorities.
4. **Raw material volatility** — Wafers and solar cells constitute 50% of raw material cost; Waaree's backward integration (Oman polysilicon, Nagpur ingot/wafer) mitigates but doesn't eliminate.
5. **China dumping** — Oversupply from China flooding global markets could pressure export realizations.

**Early warning signals:** Utilization below 50% for 2 consecutive quarters, EBITDA margin compression below 18%, order book declining QoQ.

🔒 **Score: 1/2** | Source: CARE rationale, industry analyses
