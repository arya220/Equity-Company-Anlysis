- South Korea's defence industry benefits from US technology transfer agreements India does not have
- India's procurement is fragmented (MoD, state police, paramilitary, exports) vs. Israel/Korea's concentrated buyer base

## 📐 KEY LEARNINGS FOR FRAMEWORK

1. **YoY growth vs sequential trajectory:** Q3 FY26 YoY +16.8% felt positive in isolation but was against a trough quarter. Always compare 6-8 quarter trajectory, not YoY.

2. **TTM vs prior year:** When TTM revenue is below prior full year, the business is contracting — regardless of YoY for the latest quarter. This is a high-signal data point.

3. **CFO negativity in a high-quality business:** The business isn't broken — it's just not generating cash. In defence specifically, this is often structural (long working capital cycles). But it means the balance sheet is being drawn down or diluted to fund operations, which matters for any thesis.

4. **Promoter holding trend vs point-in-time:** "Down 11.7% in 3 years" is different from "a one-time QIP diluted promoters." The former is a consistent behavior that deserves Phase 3 weight.

5. **Technical bounce vs confirmed Stage 2:** Reclaiming a declining moving average is not Stage 2 confirmation. Stage 2 requires the MA itself to be rising. This distinction matters for G4.

6. **Triage is not analysis:** A 15-minute surface scan should identify CANDIDATES for analysis, not PRIORITIES. The Pre-Flight Data Audit is the filter that converts candidates to priorities. In this case, it converted a #1 priority to an archive. The time cost (20 minutes) was entirely justified.
