## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Sky Gold Limited is a B2B gold jewellery manufacturer based in Mumbai, operating primarily in the lightweight/studded jewellery segment. The company has delivered extraordinary revenue growth — TTM revenue of ~₹5,442 Cr with a ~65% CAGR — making it one of the fastest-growing jewellery companies in India by top-line metrics.

However, this growth is entirely funded by a working capital treadmill. **CFO/EBITDA has been negative in all four reported fiscal years** (FY22: -35%, FY23: -16%, FY24: -177%, FY25: -139%). Every rupee of reported EBITDA — and then some — is consumed by expanding working capital, primarily inventory and receivables. Borrowings have ballooned to ₹814 Cr, and debtor days have deteriorated from 21 to 47 days.

The company carries an IND A credit rating and trades at ~23x P/E, which appears optically cheap vs. growth. But P/E is meaningless when cash flow is structurally negative — there is no free cash to value. The Hard DQ on CFO/EBITDA fires automatically, resulting in a **STRONG AVOID** verdict.

## 5. KEY RISKS

1. **Zero cash generation** — CFO/EBITDA negative all 4 years is the single most dangerous financial pattern in the framework. Revenue growth without cash flow is accounting growth, not economic value creation.
2. **Working capital treadmill** — Every rupee of revenue growth requires incremental WC funding. Borrowings at ₹814 Cr and rising. If credit tightens or lenders pull back, the growth model collapses.
3. **Debtor days deterioration** (21 → 47 days) — Suggests the company is extending credit to sustain growth. This is a classic red flag in B2B businesses — revenue quality is degrading even as quantity grows.
4. **Razor-thin OPM (<6%)** — Leaves zero margin of safety. Any input cost spike, volume disruption, or competitive pressure immediately hits bottom line.
5. **Gold price/inventory risk** — B2B gold jewellery businesses carry significant commodity risk. An adverse gold price move on unsold inventory can wipe out multiple quarters of operating profit.
