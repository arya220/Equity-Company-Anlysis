## 2. Thematic Context

*Theme in one sentence:* Indian telecom passive infrastructure sharing — a mature, consolidated segment with residual 5G densification / 4G-to-5G loading upside, structurally constrained by a 2-paying-telco + 1-insourcing-Jio customer base.

*Maturity:* Late innings. Industry tower count ~800K+; most coverage saturation achieved. Incremental growth is densification, not greenfield.

*Global precedent:* American Tower / SBA / Crown Castle provide the towerco REIT blueprint. **Key differences:** US has 4+ paying telcos ensuring renewal pricing discipline; India effectively has 2 (Vi survival still govt-dependent) plus Jio (in-sourcing preference). This explains why Indus trades at 6.07x EV/EBITDA vs AMT/SBA at 18-22x — and this gap is structural, not a valuation gap that will mechanically close.

*Policy:* Digital Bharat, RoW 2024 rules supportive but not tower-specific. AGR relief was Vi-specific, one-off.

*Theme risk:* Vi fragility recurring (probability of AGR relief proving insufficient: moderate); Jio in-sourcing expanding; 5-10Y tech substitution (small cells, satellite IoT).
