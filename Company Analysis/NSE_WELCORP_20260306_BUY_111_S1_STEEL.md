## PHASE 1: BUSINESS ORIENTATION (20 Points)

### 1.1 Business Model Clarity — 🔒 Score: 5/5

Welspun Corp operates across clearly documented segments:

**Steel Products segment** (~95% revenue): Large-diameter welded line pipes (HSAW, LSAW, ERW) for oil/gas/water; Ductile Iron (DI) pipes for water distribution; Stainless Steel pipes & bars (via subsidiary WSSL); TMT bars & billets.

**Others segment** (~5% revenue): Sintex-BAPL — water tanks and plastic pipes (B2C consumer brand).

Revenue split is documented in quarterly results and investor presentations. Monetization is through manufacturing and sale of pipes with coating/value-add services. The model is capital-intensive but structurally differentiated by global manufacturing presence (India, US, Saudi Arabia, UK).

*Source: FY25 Annual Report, Q2 FY26 Investor Presentation, Screener.in*

### 1.2 Industry Positioning — 🔒 Score: 4/5

Welspun Corp ranks among the top 3 line pipe manufacturers globally. It is the largest in India for large-diameter SAW pipes and one of the largest in the US (Little Rock, Arkansas facility). EBITDA margins have expanded from 7% (FY22) to 12-14% (FY25/FY26), now above sector median. Pricing power exists in export/US markets where Welspun is a local preferred supplier to Fortune 100 O&G companies (Shell, Aramco, TC Energy, Kinder Morgan, ExxonMobil). However, domestic DI and water pipe markets are more commoditized and competitive.

*Source: CARE rating report Dec 2025, Q2 FY26 Concall, BSE AR FY25*

### 1.3 Recent Developments — 🔒 Score: 5/5

Significant positive developments confirmed in BSE filings with quantified impact:

1. **US subsidiary received $715M orders** (Oct 2025) — largest in company history, for data center gas pipelines.
2. **Global order book at record ₹23,600 Cr** (Jan 2026) — US visibility till FY28.
3. **Credit rating upgraded** to CARE AA+ (Aug 2025).
