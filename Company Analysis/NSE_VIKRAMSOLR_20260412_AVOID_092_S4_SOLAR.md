## 2. THEMATIC CONTEXT

**Theme:** India's solar energy transition — 500 GW RE target by 2030 vs ~75 GW current installed solar capacity. Domestic manufacturing mandate (BCD on imports, PLI scheme, Domestic Content Requirement) creating a structural shift from import-dependence to domestic module + cell manufacturing.

**Maturity:** Early-mid innings — solar penetration below 20% of India's 500 GW target; organized domestic manufacturing capacity still building; significant runway remaining (~85 GW/year required through 2030).

**Global Precedent:** China's solar manufacturing scale-up provides a directional precedent. Key differences: India lacks upstream polysilicon/wafer capacity, has different grid infrastructure challenges, and faces smaller initial domestic demand base. Enabling conditions (policy support, capital availability) are emerging but incomplete.

**Policy Tailwind:** Active — PLI Scheme for solar modules/cells, Basic Customs Duty (BCD) on imports, MNRE efficiency thresholds, Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO) framework. Quantified government target: 500 GW by 2030.

**Theme Risk:** US CVD/AD duties (125.87%, final ruling Jul 2026) have effectively shut Indian modules out of US market via direct exports. Chinese overcapacity dumping risk in domestic market. Technology shift risk (HJT, perovskite) could render current N-TOPCon investments partially obsolete. Theme is durable but not risk-free.

However, key differences are material: (a) India lacks upstream polysilicon/wafer capacity — dependent on imports; (b) India's grid infrastructure and evacuation capacity constrain installation pace; (c) Chinese manufacturers benefit from decade-long cost curve learning that Indian manufacturers are only beginning; (d) India's domestic consumption base was smaller until recent policy push. Precedent is partial — enabling conditions exist (policy, capital) but structural gaps remain. Source: BNEF, IEA Solar PV reports.

### 0.5.4 Policy & Regulatory Tailwind — 2/2

Quantified and time-bound. Risk of policy reversal is low given cross-party consensus on renewables. Source: MNRE notifications, PLI Scheme guidelines.

### 0.5.5 Competitive Landscape — 1/1

Capital intensity rising (cell + module integration requires ₹2,000-3,000 Cr capex). New entrant threat limited by technology requirements and ALMM compliance. Source: CRISIL sector reports, company filings.

### 0.5.6 Theme Risk — 1/2
