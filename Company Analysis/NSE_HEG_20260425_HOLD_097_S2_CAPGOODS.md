## 🌐 PHASE 0.5: THEMATIC ASSESSMENT

### 0.5.1 Theme Identification — 2/2
**Theme:** Global decarbonization-driven shift from Blast Furnace (BF) to Electric Arc Furnace (EAF) steelmaking, where graphite electrodes are an indispensable consumable. Same steel produced via EAF emits ~1/5th the carbon vs BF route — structurally driven by climate goals, CBAM, and replacement of aging Western capacity. Source: Q3 FY26 concall (Ravi Jhunjhunwala, verified verbatim).

🔒 **Score: 2/2**

### 0.5.2 Theme Maturity — 2/3
- US already 70%+ EAF (mature precedent)
- EU ~45-50% EAF (mid-cycle)
- World ex-US: ~30-35% EAF, expected 35-40% by 2030
- 110 MT new EAF capacity by 2030 = 200kt incremental electrode demand vs current ~700kt market = ~28% expansion
- Mid innings — structural runway exists but US already mature

🔒 **Score: 2/3**

### 0.5.3 Global Precedent Check — 1/2
- **Precedent:** US transitioned to 70%+ EAF over 25 years driven by mini-mill economics + scrap availability
- **Enabling conditions:** Cheap natural gas, abundant scrap, strong Section 232 tariffs on imports, regulatory pressure
- **KEY DIFFERENCES (mandatory):**
  1. India's domestic steel is dominated by BF-route integrated producers (Tata, JSW, SAIL) — slower transition
  2. Coal-based electricity in India favors BF economics
  3. CBAM affects exports only, not domestic Indian production
  4. India's scrap availability is constrained vs US
- Precedent applies more to HEG's EXPORT markets (US, EU) than domestic — and HEG is 2/3 export

🔒 **Score: 1/2** (precedent valid for 2/3 of revenue, but not full)

### 0.5.4 Policy & Regulatory Tailwind — 2/2
- **CBAM (EU)** active — confirmed carbon border tax on imported steel from Q4 FY26
- **US Section 232** — tariffs on Chinese steel push Western capacity toward EAF
- **US tariff on Indian electrodes reduced 50% → 18%** (manageable per management)
- **India PLI** — specialty steel and lithium-ion cell PLI schemes support TACC anode pivot
- Active, quantified policy support

🔒 **Score: 2/2**

### 0.5.5 Competitive Landscape — 1/1
- Major UHP players globally: HEG, Graphite India, GrafTech, Resonac (Japan), Tokai Carbon, Showa Denko
- **No new greenfield plant since HEG's own 1976 commissioning** — a 50-year structural barrier
- 2-3 Western capacities (30-40kt each) shut down in last 3 years
- HEG has world's largest single-site (100kt) — top 3-5 globally
- Chinese capacity is overhang but mostly non-UHP

🔒 **Score: 1/1**

### 0.5.6 Theme Risk — 1/2
- **Durability:** Multi-decade carbon transition is structural
- **Risks identified:**
  1. Chinese UHP quality catching up (1-2 Chinese players now making UHP) — medium probability over 3-5 years
  2. Steel demand cyclicality independent of EAF transition
  3. EAF additions could be delayed (mgmt acknowledges 6-month variability)
  4. Carbon policy reversal (low probability)
- 1-2 identifiable risks could slow but not kill the theme

🔒 **Score: 1/2**
