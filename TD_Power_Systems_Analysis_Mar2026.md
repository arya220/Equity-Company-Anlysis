## PHASE 1: BUSINESS ORIENTATION (20 Points)

### 1.1 Business Model Clarity — 5 pts

TD Power Systems is a pure-play manufacturer of AC generators (1 MW to 200 MW) serving steam, gas, hydro, wind, diesel, and geothermal applications. The company sells custom-designed generators to global OEMs (~45 OEMs across 110 countries). Revenue segments per FY25 annual report:

- **Manufacturing Business (generators + motors):** ~95%+ of revenue. Generators for gas turbines/engines (largest growing segment driven by data centers), steam turbines, hydro turbines, diesel engines, and wind. Motors (traction + industrial) are a smaller but growing sub-segment.
- **Project Business (EPC):** Via subsidiary DF Power Systems — small and being deprioritized.
- **Spares & Refurbishment:** Recurring revenue stream.

Export revenue has grown to ~65-75% of total (up from ~40-50% four years ago). Capital-light model with custom engineering capability as a differentiator.

**🔒 LOCK Score: 5/5** | Source: FY25 AR, CRISIL April 2025, Q3 FY26 investor presentation

---

### 1.2 Industry Positioning — 5 pts

TDPS is one of the leading manufacturers of AC generators in the 1-50 MW segment in India, and among the few globally that manufacture across all prime mover types. EBITDA margins have expanded from 7% (FY20) to 18-19% (FY25-TTM), above most domestic peers in the heavy electrical space. The company has pricing power via customization for global OEMs (Siemens, Voith, Innio, Triveni Turbine etc.). It is not a pure commodity player — each generator is custom-engineered.

Peer margin comparison (approximate):
- TDPS: 18% EBITDA margin (FY25)
- Triveni Turbine: ~25% EBITDA margin
- BHEL: ~8-10% EBITDA margin
- Kirloskar Electric: Loss-making/marginal

TDPS is below Triveni on margins but above most heavy electrical peers. Has clear differentiation via global OEM relationships.

**Recovery timeline:** Profits turned positive FY19-FY20 and accelerated from FY21. Revenue fully recovered by FY22.

The company survived a brutal 4-year loss period without dilution, maintained its order base, and emerged with a structurally better business (higher export mix, wider margins). Communication during stress was relatively straightforward based on available concall records.
