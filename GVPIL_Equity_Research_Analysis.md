## PHASE 1: BUSINESS ORIENTATION (20 Points)

### 1.1 Business Model Clarity — Score: 4/5

GE Power India is engaged in engineering, procurement, construction and servicing of thermal power plant equipment. Revenue streams:

**Continuing Operations (post-restructuring):**
- **Core Services** (~60-70% of continuing revenue): Spare parts supply, field repair services, and technical advisory for installed base of boilers, turbines, generators, AQC systems across NTPC, state utilities, and IPPs. Expanding to non-GEPIL assets (53% of core orders in Q3 FY26 from non-GEPIL base).
- **Upgrades** (~20-30%): Turbine upgrades, boiler modernization (e.g., Wanakbori ₹243 Cr order), efficiency improvement services.
- **FGD** (winding down): Flue gas desulphurization — two major contracts (Jaypee Bina & Nigrie, ₹775 Cr) terminated in Q2 FY26.

**Discontinued/Demerging:**
- **Gas/Hydro** business: Discontinued in FY26. Being wound down.
- **Durgapur Facility**: Boiler pressure parts, piping, coal mills manufacturing — demerging to JSW Energy (NCLT pending, expected H2 CY2026). FY25 revenue ₹530 Cr (5.1% of total), negative net worth (-₹204 Cr).

Monetization is EPC project + services revenue model. Capital-light services model emerging. Segment contribution not precisely broken out in filed results but directionally clear from concall.

**Source:** BSE Annual Report FY25; Q3 FY26 Investor Presentation; Q2 FY26 Concall Transcript

### 1.2 Industry Positioning — Score: 3/5

GE Power India is one of India's leading thermal power equipment and services players, backed by GE Vernova's global technology. However:

- **Margins vs peers**: Consolidated OPM was negative for FY22-FY25. TTM OPM is 10% (recovering). Peers like BHEL, Thermax, Triveni Turbines operate at 10-20% EBITDA margins. GEPIL is below median on a trailing basis.
- **Market position**: Strong in services/spares for GE-supplied installed base. Expanding to non-GE plants. But thermal power equipment is a structurally challenged sector — India's capacity additions are increasingly renewable-led.
- **Competitive landscape**: Intense competition from BHEL (dominant), Siemens Energy, Doosan, L&T. Services segment has more defensibility due to OEM lock-in (GE proprietary parts/technology).
- **Pricing power**: Limited in EPC (fixed-price contracts, input cost vulnerability per ICRA). Better in services/spares (OEM exclusivity).

The pivot to services improves positioning but the company is currently below-median on financial metrics vs listed peers.
