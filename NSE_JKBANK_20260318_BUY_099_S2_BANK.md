## PHASE 1: BUSINESS ORIENTATION — 15/20

### 1.1 Business Model Clarity — 4/5

J&K Bank is a mid-sized private sector scheduled commercial bank, the only private bank designated as RBI's agent for the UT Government of J&K and Ladakh. The bank was incorporated in 1938, headquartered in Srinagar.

**Revenue Segments (H1 FY25):**
- Retail Banking: 60%
- Treasury: 21%
- Corporate Banking: 19%

**Network:** 1,008 branches, 1,436 ATMs, 169 CRMs across 18 states and 4 UTs as of Dec 2025. Of 1,019 branches (Jun 2025 data), 878 are within J&K/Ladakh (86%). Rural branches constitute 54.4% of total network.

**Monetization:** Standard banking model — NII from lending spread, fee income from services, treasury income from investments. Deposit base of ₹1.56 lakh Cr funds a loan book of ₹1.16 lakh Cr.

Source: BSE Annual Report FY25, CARE Rating Report Sep 2025, Screener.in

🔒 **LOCK Score: 4/5**

---

### 1.2 Industry Positioning — 3/5

J&K Bank holds a dominant position within J&K/Ladakh — majority credit and deposit market share in the UT. However, compared to larger private banks (HDFC Bank, ICICI, Kotak), the bank is mid-sized with moderate margins.

**NIM:** 3.62% (Q3 FY26) / 3.64% (9M FY26) — competitive for a regional bank. Industry-leading NIMs are 4%+ (Kotak, HDFC Bank).

**Cost-to-Income:** 57.73% (FY25) — improving but elevated vs top-quartile private banks (40-50% range).

**ROE:** 16% (FY25) — strong, comparable to top private banks.

**Moat:** Geographic monopoly in J&K creates sticky deposit base and government business franchise. However, this same concentration limits scalability.

### 3.1 Distress Test — 7/10

**Stress periods identified:**
- **FY17:** Net loss ₹1,633 Cr. GNPA spiked to ~11%. Driven by corporate NPAs in industrial sector.
- **FY20:** Net loss ₹1,183 Cr. Second wave of NPA stress. Govt injected fresh capital.

**What management said during stress:** During FY17-FY20, management communication was partially transparent. The bank acknowledged NPA issues but was slow to recognize the full extent of stress. **What actually happened:** Recovery took 3-4 years. PAT turned positive in FY21 (₹428 Cr), then scaled to ₹2,082 Cr by FY25. GNPA declined from 10%+ to 3.00%. The turnaround under MD&CEO Amitava Chatterjee has been genuine and sustained.

**Assessment:** Management navigated severe stress, but it took an extended period with significant Govt support. No value-destructive external raises beyond Govt infusion. Communication was adequate but not exemplary during stress.
